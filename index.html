About this site

This site is powered by Jekyll, a static site generator written by Tom Preston-Werner and maintained by Nick Quaranto, and GitHub pages, a hosted service based on Jekyll. In designing the site, I leaned heavily on Compass, the CSS framework which has rapidly made itself indispensable around me and my projects. As a consequence of this, the styles are written by me in Sass, then compiled locally into CSS stylesheets with a post-commit hook. Blueprint, Baseline and Sass, and fancied with jQuery and jQuery UI.

As this is not in any way a "production" site but a personal dalliance, I have felt free to use HTML5 and CSS3 to the extent to which they are useful or interesting to me. Everything looks fine in Firefox 3.6 on Linux, and I've made cursory attempts to verify that its not a crime scene in Epiphany (Webkit) on Linux and also in Safari on the Mac. If it does not look usable - nay, stunning - I can only urge you to support modern web standards and use a compliant browser.
